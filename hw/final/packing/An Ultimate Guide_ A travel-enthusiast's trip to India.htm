Planning a trip to India?

I have lived in Bangalore, India for 10 years & have visited most of the major cities. Here is a guide from a native who moved to India as an expat.

Must-See Cities

New Delhi (दिल्ली)

As the capital of India, and home to one of the seven wonders of the modern world, the Taj Mahal, New Delhi is a must when visiting India. It is in the center of the country, and a huge airline hub so, a convenient first stop on your trip. Here you can visit the India Gate, the Qutab Minar, Red Fort, Jama Masjid and several other iconic historical sights. You can walk around and eat authentic cuisine like Mutton Seekh Kabab at Chandni Chowk, the main street of old Delhi, a shocking contrast to the wide, orderly streets of New Delhi. Cars, cycle rickshaws, hand-pulled carts, pedestrians, and animals all compete for space.

Mumbai (मुम्बई)

Mumbai is the most socially and economically developed city in India. It is home to all the Bollywood celebrities, and some of the richest people in the world. However, this is the cause to the large income gap between the rich and the poor. As, it is also known for the largest slums in the world. Personally, visiting a city like Mumbai not only is a culturally eye-opening experience, but also provides a tourist with perspective. Mumbai is much like a hustling bustling city in America. You must sit by Marina Drive eating a samosa, visit the elephanta caves, eat the world famous Pav Bhaji and Goli Pav, and also explore the thriving nightlife if you are 21+!

Exploring the Attractions

Going Outside the Major Cities

Make sure you visit some of the minor, less touristy and less populated cities. Some of these hold the real gems of the country, in terms of monuments, landmarks, culture and cuisine.

The Golden Temple: Amritsar

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, meaning "abode of God" or Darbār Sahib, meaning "exalted court", is a Gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is the holiest Gurdwara and the most important pilgrimage site of Sikhism. The Golden Temple has a unique Sikh architecture. Built at a level lower than the surrounding land level, The Gurudwara teaches the lesson of egalitarianism and humility. The four entrances of this holy shrine from all four directions, signify that people belonging to every walk of life are equally welcome.

Amer Fort: Jaipur

Amer Fort is a fort located in Amer, Rajasthan, India. Amer is a town with an area of 4 square kilometres located 11 kilometres from Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan. Located high on a hill, it is the principal tourist attraction in Jaipur. A popular way of reaching the top of Amber Fort is to ride on an elephant from the car park to Jaleb Chowk.

Rann of Kutch: Gujarat

This is the Rann of Kutch, home to India's largest salt plain, in the Kutch district of the westernmost state of Gujarat. The region is divided into two distinct areas -- the Great Rann and the Little Rann -- each offering different experiences. (Rann means "desert" in Hindi.) The former is best known for its spectacular landscapes, while the latter destination is a hub of wildlife and salt farming.

Travel Tips

Packing Tips

**Please note: While shorts may be comfortable and preferable in some places due to warm temperatures, only wear shorts that are mid-thigh length or longer. It is culturally respectful. Except in major cities where western influence is more apparent, shorts are rarely worn in India. “Short shorts” and spaghetti straps are inappropriate.

Ziploc Bags: These are perfect for storing small items and items that could leak into your luggage as you travel alot.

Baggage: Review the Airline Baggage Rules and TSA Guidelines. Most checked luggage cannot be over 50lbs.

Laundry: Most hotels have laundry service. Laundry dropped off in the morning is finished by the evening. If you plan on doing this, make sure to budget extra spending money for the service. In addition, you could pack Woolite or a small container of detergent and do your own laundry.

Do I need a Visa?

Yes. You need a travel Visa. Apply at least 3 months in advance.

Electronics and Converters

Indian hotels have hairdryers. No converters are necessary due to the fact that modern electronics auto-matically make the conversion. No adapters are needed

Will My Cell Phone Work in India?

Yes. Call your cell service provider to let them know that you will be traveling in India and find out about the best international plan for your needs. Be careful about using cell data in China. Use the internet when you have wifi access.

Do I Need Vaccinations to go to India?

Vaccinations are NOT REQUIRED for India. The Hepatitis A (food-borne illness) vaccination is recommended. Please speak with your doctor for a list of other recommended vaccinations, many of which you may already have had.

Probiotics

Consider taking a probiotic, active yeasts and live bacteria that are good for your digestive system, for a week or two before departure and for the duration of the trip.

Can I Drink the Water in India?

No. Even the locals do not drink the water. Most hotels have two complimentary bottles of water per day. It is fine to use the water to brush your teeth, wash up and shower. If you are concerned, however, you could boil the water in the room for tooth brushing. There is a kettle provided in every hotel room.

Culture Shock

  • Locals will stare at you, take pictures of you and ask you to take pictures with them. Enjoy your 5 minutes of fame as a tourist in India!
  • Squat toilets. Enough said. Try not to use public restrooms. Bring some pocket packets of tissues.

The Language

In most of India, Hindi is the most common language. Below are very useful words and phrases to use while comminicating with locals.

Greetings

Hello

नमस्ते (namaste)

My Name Is

मेरा नाम ___ है (mera naam ___ hai)

Thank You

धन्यवाद (dhanyavaad)

You're Welcome

आपका स्वागत है (aapaka svaagat hai)

Okay

ठीक है (theek hai)

Shopping

How Much?

कितना?(kithana?)

Too Expensive

बहुत ज्यादा (bahut jyada)

Make it a little cheaper

इसे कम करो (ise kam karo)

General and Helpful

Where is the bathroom

bathroom कहां है (bathroom kaha hai)

I Don't Understand

मुझे समझ नहीं आया (samaj nahi aaya)
